Welcome, here are some projects of mine

My main project is a three-part institutional and intellectual history of The New School’s economics department. A 121-page draft of Part 1 is available here:

Economics at The New School — 2024 draft (Google Drive)

Here are some other projects and pages on this site:

  1. TNSE — Economics at The New School A three-part institutional and intellectual history of The New School’s economics department from its 1919 founding to the present. Part 1 (121 pages drafted) covers 1919–1933. Parts 2 and 3 extend the history to 1960 and include an oral history component with 84 structured interviews.

  2. Research Empirical work on public finance, tax revenue cyclicality, historical balance-of-payments data, and a replication and extension of the empirical data in Anwar Shaikh’s Capitalism (2016). Includes original figures built from U.S. Treasury and BEA data.

  3. realecon.org A public website built as a companion to Anwar Shaikh’s work in economics. Hosts author bibliographies, forum discussions, and resources for heterodox and classical political economy.

  4. HMDA Explore An interactive 33-year Home Mortgage Disclosure Act explorer built during my Schwartz Center research assistantship at The New School. Please allow the application a moment to spin up.

  5. AI Tools Open-source research infrastructure for AI-assisted empirical economics. Protocols for PDF processing, reproducible economic data construction, and pipeline architecture for empirical research.

  6. Projects Public code repositories and data tools, including FreeNIC (banking regulatory data 1863–2026), Foodberg (historical food prices), and a Westchester County sidewalk-accessibility analysis.

Thank you and let me know if you have any questions!
